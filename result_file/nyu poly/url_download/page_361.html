Samuel Estreicher

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search

Samuel Estreicher (b 1948) is Dwight D. Opperman Professor of Law at New York University School of Law, director of its Center for Labor and Employment[1] and co-director of its Institute of Judicial Administration.[2] He has published scores of articles and several books on labor law, employment law, employment discrimination law, U.S. foreign relations law, international law,and Supreme Court decisionmaking.

Biography[edit]

Born in the displaced-persons camp at Bergen-Belsen, Germany, in 1948, Estreicher and his parents came to the U.S. two years later, settling first in Allentown, Pennsylvania, and then in the Bronx, New York.[citation needed] Estreicher graduated from Brooklyn Technical High School in 1966.[citation needed] He received his A.B. from Columbia College in 1970, his M.S. (Industrial Relations) from Cornell University in 1974 and his J.D. from Columbia Law School the following year.[citation needed] At Columbia, he was editor-in-chief of the Columbia Law Review.[citation needed]

After law school Estreicher clerked for the late Harold Leventhal of the United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit, practiced with a union-side law firm, and then clerked for the late Lewis F. Powell, Jr. of the United States Supreme Court.[3] In 1978 Estreicher joined the faculty of New York University School of Law, where he teaches labor and employment law, appellate advocacy and courses in administrative law, foreign relations and international law.[citation needed]

Mr. Estreicher is the former Secretary of the Labor and Employment Law Section of the American Bar Association, a former chair of the Committee on Labor and Employment Law of the Association of the Bar for the City of New York, and chief reporter of the new Restatement of Employment Law, unanimously approved by the membership of the American Law Institute in May 2014.[4] He has lectured at Columbia Law, UCLA, Chicago-Kent,[5] Case Western and Cleveland State law schools, testified before Congress and the Commission on the Future of U.S. Worker-Management Relations, and organized dozens of workshops for federal and state judges, U.S. Department of Labor lawyers, NLRB lawyers, EEOC lawyers, court law clerks, employment mediators and practitioners generally.

Mr. Estreicher is a member of the arbitration/mediation panels of the American Arbitration Association and Center for Public Resources, and is a Fellow of the College of Labor and Employment Lawyers.[citation needed] In 2010, the Labor and Employment Relations Association (successor to the Industrial Relations Research Association) awarded Estreicher its Susan C. Eaton Award for Outstanding Scholar-Practitioner of the year.[citation needed]

Estreicher has been counsel to a number of major law firms. He also maintains an active appellate practice.
